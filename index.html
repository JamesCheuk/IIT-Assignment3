As a reflector, James is a cautious learner. He likes to take his time to collect and analyse information to reach a conclusion. James does not necessarily need to put into action what he has learned to feel comfortable.
Andrew’s dominant learning style is quite the opposite of James. To take in the information and understand it, Andrew finds it necessary to observe and learn in the real world instead of practice the theory.
As we work together, we will need to be more conscious of our learning styles to ensure that we understand the work that needs to be delivered and how we can support each other through the various activities that are to be completed.
